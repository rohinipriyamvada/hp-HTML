The Founding of Hogwarts:

Hogwarts was founded in the 10th century by four of the most powerful and respected witches and wizards of their time: Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin. They each had their own vision of the type of students they wanted to educate and created the four houses of Hogwartsâ€”Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, and Slytherin. While Gryffindor valued bravery and courage, Hufflepuff embraced loyalty and hard work. Ravenclaw sought out intelligence and wisdom, whereas Slytherin preferred students with ambition and cunning.

The founders worked together in harmony for many years, but conflicts eventually arose, particularly between Salazar Slytherin and the others. Slytherin believed that only pure-blood wizards should be admitted to the school, while the others disagreed, believing magical ability was more important than blood status. This disagreement led Slytherin to leave the school.
