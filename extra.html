To dear young wizards and witches:

Sorting Hat:

Created by the four founders, the hat was enchanted to determine which house best suits each student. When a new student arrives at Hogwarts, the Sorting Hat is placed on their head, and after assessing their personality, qualities, and potential, it assigns them to Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, or Slytherin. The Sorting Hat is not just an ordinary hat; it possesses intelligence and the ability to speak. It even composes and sings a new song each year at the Sorting Ceremony, offering insights into the history and values of each house. The hat considers the student’s personal traits, but in some cases, it also takes their preferences into account.

Quidditch:

Quidditch is the most popular sport in the wizarding world and a beloved tradition at Hogwarts. It is a fast-paced, high-flying game played on broomsticks, combining elements of speed, strategy, and teamwork. The game is played between two teams of seven players, each aiming to score points while defending their own goalposts. The game is played with four balls: the Quaffle, the Bludgers, and the Golden Snitch. Chasers try to score goals with the Quaffle, Beaters use bats to deflect the aggressive Bludgers, the Keeper guards the goalposts, and the Seeker's task is to catch the Golden Snitch, which earns their team 150 points and usually ends the game. The fast-moving Snitch makes the Seeker’s role crucial, often determining the outcome of the match.

The Forbidden forest: A place of mystery and danger

**Students are strictly prohibited from entering the forbidden forest.**
The Forbidden Forest, located on the outskirts of Hogwarts, is a vast, dark, and eerie woodland that is strictly off-limits to students unless accompanied by a teacher. It is home to a variety of magical creatures, some friendly and others highly dangerous. Among its inhabitants are centaurs, giant spiders like Aragog and his colony, unicorns, thestrals, and even werewolves. The forest is also rumored to be a hiding place for dark magic and has witnessed many significant events in wizarding history.

Proper use of magic:

Any use of magic outside hogwarts is not allowed unless it is necessary. The International Statute of Secrecy, established in 1692, strictly forbids the use of magic in the presence of Muggles to protect the wizarding world from exposure. This rule ensures that magical society remains hidden and prevents chaos or fear among non-magical people. The Ministry of Magic enforces this law, and violations can result in serious consequences, including warnings, fines, or even expulsion from institution. Young witches and wizards under the age of 17 are especially monitored through the Trace, a magical detection system that alerts the Ministry if magic is performed outside of school. However, exceptions exist, such as self-defense or emergencies. This law plays a significant role in maintaining harmony between the magical and non-magical worlds, ensuring that wizards live discreetly while preserving their traditions and safety. The secrecy surrounding magic is a fundamental aspect of wizarding life, shaping the interactions between the two worlds throughout history.
